
# Moral Domain

## Moral Judgments and Evaluations

**Domain:** Moral  
**Subdomain:** Moral Judgments and Evaluations

### Scenario 1: Ethical Assessment and Moral Reasoning

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralEvaluator engages in #EthicalAssessment of #MoralSituations by applying #MoralCriteria and #EthicalStandards through #MoralReasoning processes. The evaluator considers #MoralFactors, #EthicalContext, and #MoralConsequences to form #MoralJudgments and reach #EthicalConclusions.


**Elements:**

- **MoralEvaluator** (Entity): Person making systematic moral assessments and ethical judgments
- **EthicalAssessment** (Process): Systematic evaluation of moral dimensions of situations or actions
- **MoralSituations** (Entity): Circumstances involving ethical considerations and moral choices
- **MoralCriteria** (Entity): Standards used to judge moral rightness or wrongness
- **EthicalStandards** (Entity): Principles that define morally acceptable behavior
- **MoralReasoning** (Process): Cognitive processes used to evaluate ethical issues
- **MoralFactors** (Entity): Elements that influence moral evaluation (intentions, consequences, context)
- **EthicalContext** (State): Situational circumstances affecting moral evaluation
- **MoralConsequences** (Entity): Outcomes and effects considered in moral evaluation
- **MoralJudgments** (Entity): Conclusions about moral rightness or wrongness
- **EthicalConclusions** (Entity): Final determinations reached through moral reasoning

**Relations:**

- MoralEvaluator engages in EthicalAssessment of MoralSituations
- EthicalAssessment applies MoralCriteria and EthicalStandards
- MoralEvaluator uses MoralReasoning to evaluate situations
- MoralReasoning considers MoralFactors and EthicalContext
- MoralFactors include analysis of MoralConsequences
- EthicalAssessment produces MoralJudgments
- MoralReasoning leads to EthicalConclusions

### Scenario 2: Moral Responsibility and Blame Attribution

**Description:** In this scenario, a #ResponsibilityAssessor evaluates #MoralResponsibility and #BlameAttribution for #MoralActions by examining #AgentIntentions, #CausalContribution, and #ControlFactors. The assessor considers #MitigatingCircumstances and #AggravatingFactors to determine #MoralAccountability and assign appropriate #MoralCredit or #MoralBlame.


**Elements:**

- **ResponsibilityAssessor** (Entity): Person evaluating moral responsibility and assigning blame or credit
- **MoralResponsibility** (Entity): Degree to which someone is accountable for moral outcomes
- **BlameAttribution** (Process): Assignment of moral responsibility for negative outcomes
- **MoralActions** (Entity): Behaviors with moral significance requiring responsibility assessment
- **AgentIntentions** (Entity): Purposes and motivations behind moral actions
- **CausalContribution** (Quality): Degree to which an agent caused moral outcomes
- **ControlFactors** (Entity): Elements affecting an agent's ability to control their actions
- **MitigatingCircumstances** (Entity): Factors that reduce moral responsibility
- **AggravatingFactors** (Entity): Factors that increase moral responsibility
- **MoralAccountability** (State): Overall responsibility assigned to moral agents
- **MoralCredit** (Entity): Positive moral responsibility for good outcomes
- **MoralBlame** (Entity): Negative moral responsibility for harmful outcomes

**Relations:**

- ResponsibilityAssessor evaluates MoralResponsibility and BlameAttribution for MoralActions
- Assessment examines AgentIntentions, CausalContribution, and ControlFactors
- MitigatingCircumstances reduce assigned MoralResponsibility
- AggravatingFactors increase assigned MoralResponsibility
- ResponsibilityAssessor determines overall MoralAccountability
- MoralAccountability results in assignment of MoralCredit or MoralBlame
- AgentIntentions influence degree of BlameAttribution

### Scenario 3: Moral Conflict Resolution and Ethical Dilemmas

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralArbitrator addresses #MoralConflicts and #EthicalDilemmas involving #CompetingValues and #ConflictingDuties. The arbitrator uses #MoralBalancing and #EthicalPrioritization to resolve #ValueTensions and find #MoralSolutions that address #EthicalTradeoffs and achieve #MoralResolution.

**Elements:**

- **MoralArbitrator** (Entity): Person resolving moral conflicts and ethical dilemmas
- **MoralConflicts** (Entity): Situations where moral principles or values clash
- **EthicalDilemmas** (Entity): Complex situations with competing moral demands
- **CompetingValues** (Entity): Different moral principles that conflict in specific situations
- **ConflictingDuties** (Entity): Moral obligations that cannot all be fulfilled simultaneously
- **MoralBalancing** (Process): Weighing competing moral considerations
- **EthicalPrioritization** (Process): Determining which moral values take precedence
- **ValueTensions** (State): Stress created by conflicting moral demands
- **MoralSolutions** (Entity): Resolutions that address ethical conflicts
- **EthicalTradeoffs** (Entity): Compromises necessary to resolve moral conflicts
- **MoralResolution** (State): Successful resolution of ethical conflicts

**Relations:**

- MoralArbitrator addresses MoralConflicts and EthicalDilemmas
- MoralConflicts involve CompetingValues and ConflictingDuties
- EthicalDilemmas create ValueTensions requiring resolution
- MoralArbitrator uses MoralBalancing and EthicalPrioritization
- MoralBalancing weighs CompetingValues against each other
- EthicalPrioritization determines which ConflictingDuties take precedence
- Resolution involves accepting necessary EthicalTradeoffs
- MoralSolutions achieve overall MoralResolution

## Rights and Duties and Justice

**Domain:** Moral  
**Subdomain:** Rights and Duties and Justice

### Scenario 1: Rights Recognition and Entitlement Claims

**Description:** In this scenario, a #RightsAdvocate identifies and defends #FundamentalRights and #MoralEntitlements of #RightsHolders, recognizing #RightsClaims and understanding #EntitlementBases. The advocate works to ensure #RightsProtection and #RightsRespect while addressing #RightsViolations and promoting #RightsFulfillment.


**Elements:**

- **RightsAdvocate** (Entity): Person working to recognize, defend, and promote rights
- **FundamentalRights** (Entity): Basic entitlements considered essential for human dignity
- **MoralEntitlements** (Entity): Claims to treatment or resources based on moral standing
- **RightsHolders** (Entity): Individuals or groups who possess rights
- **RightsClaims** (Entity): Assertions of entitlement to specific rights
- **EntitlementBases** (Entity): Foundations that justify rights claims (humanity, citizenship, etc.)
- **RightsProtection** (Process): Actions taken to safeguard rights from violation
- **RightsRespect** (Process): Recognition and honoring of others' rights
- **RightsViolations** (Entity): Actions that infringe upon or deny legitimate rights
- **RightsFulfillment** (Process): Positive actions to ensure rights are realized

**Relations:**

- RightsAdvocate identifies FundamentalRights and MoralEntitlements of RightsHolders
- RightsHolders make RightsClaims based on EntitlementBases
- RightsAdvocate works to ensure RightsProtection and RightsRespect
- RightsProtection prevents RightsViolations
- RightsRespect involves recognition of legitimate RightsClaims
- RightsAdvocate addresses RightsViolations when they occur
- RightsFulfillment realizes FundamentalRights in practice

### Scenario 2: Duty Recognition and Obligation Fulfillment

**Description:** In this scenario, a #DutyBearer recognizes #MoralObligations and #EthicalDuties toward #ObligationRecipients, understanding #DutyBases and #ObligationSources. The duty bearer engages in #DutyFulfillment and #ObligationCompliance while managing #DutyConflicts and understanding #ObligationLimits.


**Elements:**

- **DutyBearer** (Entity): Person who has moral obligations and duties toward others
- **MoralObligations** (Entity): Duties arising from moral principles and relationships
- **EthicalDuties** (Entity): Responsibilities based on ethical considerations
- **ObligationRecipients** (Entity): Those to whom duties are owed
- **DutyBases** (Entity): Sources that create moral obligations (roles, relationships, promises)
- **ObligationSources** (Entity): Origins of ethical duties and moral responsibilities
- **DutyFulfillment** (Process): Actions taken to meet moral obligations
- **ObligationCompliance** (Process): Following through on ethical duties
- **DutyConflicts** (Entity): Situations where multiple obligations compete
- **ObligationLimits** (Quality): Boundaries on the extent of moral duties

**Relations:**

- DutyBearer recognizes MoralObligations and EthicalDuties toward ObligationRecipients
- MoralObligations arise from DutyBases and ObligationSources
- DutyBearer engages in DutyFulfillment and ObligationCompliance
- DutyFulfillment involves meeting responsibilities to ObligationRecipients
- DutyBearer must manage DutyConflicts when obligations compete
- ObligationLimits define reasonable boundaries for EthicalDuties
- ObligationCompliance demonstrates respect for MoralObligations

### Scenario 3: Justice Administration and Fair Treatment

**Description:** In this scenario, a #JusticeSeeker pursues #FairTreatment and #JustOutcomes through #JusticeProcesses, applying #FairnessStandards and #JusticePrinciples. The seeker addresses #Injustices and #UnfairTreatment while promoting #ProceduralJustice and #DistributiveJustice to achieve #JusticeRealization.


**Elements:**

- **JusticeSeeker** (Entity): Person pursuing fair treatment and just outcomes
- **FairTreatment** (Quality): Equitable and impartial handling according to moral standards
- **JustOutcomes** (Entity): Results that satisfy principles of fairness and justice
- **JusticeProcesses** (Process): Systematic approaches to achieving fair outcomes
- **FairnessStandards** (Entity): Criteria for determining equitable treatment
- **JusticePrinciples** (Entity): Fundamental concepts guiding fair treatment
- **Injustices** (Entity): Unfair treatment or outcomes that violate justice principles
- **UnfairTreatment** (Process): Actions that fail to meet fairness standards
- **ProceduralJustice** (Process): Fair processes for making decisions and resolving disputes
- **DistributiveJustice** (Process): Fair allocation of resources, benefits, and burdens
- **JusticeRealization** (State): Achievement of fair treatment and just outcomes

**Relations:**

- JusticeSeeker pursues FairTreatment and JustOutcomes through JusticeProcesses
- JusticeProcesses apply FairnessStandards and JusticePrinciples
- JusticeSeeker addresses Injustices and UnfairTreatment
- ProceduralJustice ensures fair JusticeProcesses
- DistributiveJustice promotes equitable JustOutcomes
- FairnessStandards define what constitutes FairTreatment
- JusticeRealization represents successful achievement of justice goals

## Moral Emotions and Responses

**Domain:** Moral  
**Subdomain:** Moral Emotions and Responses

### Scenario 1: Moral Self-Assessment and Conscience Responses

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralAgent experiences #MoralEmotions and #ConscienceResponses to their own actions, feeling #MoralGuilt, #MoralPride, or #MoralShame based on #SelfEvaluation. The agent engages in #MoralReflection and #ConscienceListening to guide #MoralBehavior and achieve #MoralAlignment with values.

**Elements:**

- **MoralAgent** (Entity): Person experiencing and responding to their own moral emotions
- **MoralEmotions** (Entity): Feelings specifically related to moral evaluation and behavior
- **ConscienceResponses** (Process): Internal moral feedback about actions and intentions
- **MoralGuilt** (Entity): Negative emotion following perceived moral wrongdoing
- **MoralPride** (Entity): Positive emotion following perceived moral achievement
- **MoralShame** (Entity): Negative emotion about moral character or identity
- **SelfEvaluation** (Process): Assessment of one's own moral actions and character
- **MoralReflection** (Process): Deliberate consideration of moral aspects of behavior
- **ConscienceListening** (Process): Attending to internal moral guidance
- **MoralBehavior** (Process): Actions guided by moral considerations
- **MoralAlignment** (State): Consistency between actions and moral values

**Relations:**

- MoralAgent experiences MoralEmotions and ConscienceResponses to actions
- ConscienceResponses generate MoralGuilt, MoralPride, or MoralShame
- MoralEmotions arise from SelfEvaluation of actions
- MoralAgent engages in MoralReflection about behavior
- ConscienceListening provides guidance for MoralBehavior
- MoralReflection aims to achieve MoralAlignment
- MoralAlignment reduces negative MoralEmotions

### Scenario 2: Moral Responses to Others and Empathetic Reactions

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralObserver experiences #EmpathicResponses and #MoralIndignation toward others' actions, feeling #MoralAdmiration for good deeds and #MoralDisgust for wrongdoing. The observer engages in #MoralSupport and #MoralCorrection while experiencing #VicariousEmotions and #MoralSympathy.


**Elements:**

- **MoralObserver** (Entity): Person experiencing moral emotions in response to others' actions
- **EmpathicResponses** (Process): Emotional reactions that mirror others' moral experiences
- **MoralIndignation** (Entity): Anger or outrage in response to perceived moral wrongdoing
- **MoralAdmiration** (Entity): Positive emotion toward morally praiseworthy actions
- **MoralDisgust** (Entity): Negative emotion toward morally reprehensible actions
- **MoralSupport** (Process): Providing encouragement for moral behavior
- **MoralCorrection** (Process): Attempting to address or correct moral wrongdoing
- **VicariousEmotions** (Entity): Emotions experienced on behalf of others
- **MoralSympathy** (Entity): Compassionate response to others' moral struggles

**Relations:**

- MoralObserver experiences EmpathicResponses to others' moral situations
- EmpathicResponses include MoralIndignation toward wrongdoing
- MoralObserver feels MoralAdmiration for virtuous actions
- MoralDisgust arises in response to serious moral violations
- MoralObserver provides MoralSupport for ethical behavior
- MoralCorrection addresses observed moral wrongdoing
- VicariousEmotions enable MoralSympathy for others
- MoralSympathy motivates MoralSupport

### Scenario 3: Moral Repair and Forgiveness Processes

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralRepairer engages in #MoralRepair and #ForgivenessProcesses following #MoralHarm or #MoralTransgressions. The repairer seeks #MoralReconciliation through #Atonement, #Remorse, and #MoralAmends while working toward #RelationshipRestoration and #MoralHealing.


**Elements:**

- **MoralRepairer** (Entity): Person working to repair moral harm and restore relationships
- **MoralRepair** (Process): Actions taken to address and heal moral injuries
- **ForgivenessProcesses** (Process): Systematic approaches to forgiveness and reconciliation
- **MoralHarm** (Entity): Damage caused by moral wrongdoing
- **MoralTransgressions** (Entity): Actions that violate moral standards
- **MoralReconciliation** (Process): Restoration of moral relationships after wrongdoing
- **Atonement** (Process): Making amends for moral wrongdoing
- **Remorse** (Entity): Genuine regret and sorrow for moral harm caused
- **MoralAmends** (Process): Specific actions to repair moral damage
- **RelationshipRestoration** (Process): Rebuilding damaged relationships
- **MoralHealing** (Process): Recovery from moral injury and restoration of moral harmony

**Relations:**

- MoralRepairer engages in MoralRepair following MoralHarm or MoralTransgressions
- MoralRepair involves ForgivenessProcesses and seeks MoralReconciliation
- Atonement demonstrates Remorse for moral wrongdoing
- MoralRepairer makes MoralAmends to address harm
- ForgivenessProcesses enable RelationshipRestoration
- MoralAmends contribute to MoralHealing
- MoralReconciliation achieves RelationshipRestoration and MoralHealing


## Conventions and Ethical Norms and Principles

**Domain:** Moral  
**Subdomain:** Conventions and Ethical Norms and Principles

### Scenario 1: Social Convention Recognition and Compliance

**Description:** In this scenario, a #SocialActor encounters #SocialSituations governed by #SocialConventions and must demonstrate #ConventionRecognition to select appropriate #BehavioralChoices. The actor considers #SocialExpectations, #ContextualNorms, and potential #SocialConsequences to achieve #SocialAcceptability.

**Elements:**

- **SocialActor** (Entity): Person navigating socially regulated situations
- **SocialSituations** (Entity): Contexts with established behavioral expectations
- **SocialConventions** (Entity): Implicit rules governing appropriate social behavior
- **ConventionRecognition** (Event/Process): Identifying relevant social rules in situations
- **BehavioralChoices** (Entity): Available actions in social contexts
- **SocialExpectations** (Entity): Anticipated behaviors from others in the situation
- **ContextualNorms** (Entity): Situation-specific behavioral standards
- **SocialConsequences** (Entity): Results of conforming to or violating conventions
- **SocialAcceptability** (Quality): Degree to which behavior meets social standards

**Relations:**

- SocialActor encounters SocialSituations
- SocialSituations governed by SocialConventions
- SocialActor performs ConventionRecognition
- ConventionRecognition identifies SocialExpectations and ContextualNorms
- SocialActor selects BehavioralChoices based on conventions
- BehavioralChoices lead to SocialConsequences
- SocialConsequences determine SocialAcceptability

### Scenario 2: Ethical Principle Application and Moral Reasoning

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MoralAgent faces #EthicalDilemmas requiring application of #MoralPrinciples through #EthicalReasoning. The agent weighs #CompetingValues, considers #MoralObligations, and evaluates potential #EthicalOutcomes to make #MoralDecisions guided by #EthicalFrameworks.


**Elements:**

- **MoralAgent** (Entity): Person making decisions based on ethical considerations
- **EthicalDilemmas** (Entity): Situations requiring moral choice between competing goods
- **MoralPrinciples** (Entity): Abstract ethical guidelines like justice, autonomy, beneficence
- **EthicalReasoning** (Event/Process): Deliberative application of moral principles
- **CompetingValues** (Entity): Conflicting moral considerations in ethical dilemmas
- **MoralObligations** (Entity): Duties or responsibilities based on ethical principles
- **EthicalOutcomes** (Entity): Predicted consequences of moral decisions
- **MoralDecisions** (Entity): Choices made based on ethical reasoning
- **EthicalFrameworks** (Entity): Systematic approaches to moral reasoning

**Relations:**

- MoralAgent encounters EthicalDilemmas
- EthicalDilemmas involve CompetingValues
- MoralAgent applies MoralPrinciples through EthicalReasoning
- EthicalReasoning considers MoralObligations
- MoralAgent evaluates potential EthicalOutcomes
- EthicalFrameworks guide EthicalReasoning
- EthicalReasoning leads to MoralDecisions

### Scenario 3: Norm Violation and Moral Evaluation

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Observer witnesses #NormViolations where actors breach #EstablishedNorms. The observer engages in #MoralEvaluation, considering #ViolationSeverity, #IntentionalityFactors, and #CircumstantialContext to form #MoralJudgments that may lead to #SocialSanctions or #ForgivenessResponses.


**Elements:**

- **Observer** (Entity): Person evaluating others' norm-violating behavior
- **NormViolations** (Entity): Actions that breach established social or moral standards
- **EstablishedNorms** (Entity): Accepted standards of behavior in social groups
- **MoralEvaluation** (Event/Process): Assessment of the moral status of actions
- **ViolationSeverity** (Quality): Degree of harm or rule-breaking in violations
- **IntentionalityFactors** (Entity): Considerations of whether violations were deliberate
- **CircumstantialContext** (Entity): Situational factors affecting moral evaluation
- **MoralJudgments** (Entity): Conclusions about moral responsibility and blame
- **SocialSanctions** (Entity): Negative responses to norm violations
- **ForgivenessResponses** (Entity): Accepting or excusing norm violations

**Relations:**

- Observer witnesses NormViolations of EstablishedNorms
- Observer conducts MoralEvaluation of NormViolations
- MoralEvaluation considers ViolationSeverity, IntentionalityFactors, CircumstantialContext
- MoralEvaluation produces MoralJudgments
- MoralJudgments may lead to SocialSanctions or ForgivenessResponses
- SocialSanctions and ForgivenessResponses reinforce EstablishedNorms
