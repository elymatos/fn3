# Cognitive Ontology Scenarios Collection

# Physical Domain - New Scenarios

## Matter and Substances

**Domain:** Physical  
**Subdomain:** Matter and Substances

### Scenario 1: Material Property Recognition and Interaction

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Observer encounters different #Materials and recognizes their #MaterialProperties through #SensoryExamination. The Observer may perform #MaterialTesting to confirm properties and uses this knowledge to predict #MaterialBehavior in various #InteractionContexts.

**Elements:**

- **Observer** (Entity): Person examining and categorizing materials
- **Materials** (Entity): Substances with distinct physical and chemical properties
- **MaterialProperties** (Quality): Observable characteristics like hardness, texture, temperature, weight
- **SensoryExamination** (Event/Process): Using senses to assess material characteristics
- **MaterialTesting** (Event/Process): Deliberate actions to verify or discover properties
- **MaterialBehavior** (Event/Process): How materials respond to various conditions or forces
- **InteractionContexts** (State): Environmental conditions affecting material behavior

**Relations:**

- Observer conducts SensoryExamination of Materials
- SensoryExamination reveals MaterialProperties
- Observer performs MaterialTesting to verify MaterialProperties
- MaterialProperties determine MaterialBehavior
- InteractionContexts influence MaterialBehavior
- Observer predicts MaterialBehavior based on MaterialProperties

### Scenario 2: Material Composition and Structure

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MaterialAnalyst examines #CompositeSubstances to understand their #ComponentMaterials and #StructuralOrganization. The process involves identifying #MaterialLayers, #MixingPatterns, and #BondingTypes that create the overall #MaterialStructure with specific #FunctionalProperties.

**Elements:**

- **MaterialAnalyst** (Entity): Person studying material composition
- **CompositeSubstances** (Entity): Materials made from multiple components
- **ComponentMaterials** (Entity): Individual substances that make up composites
- **StructuralOrganization** (State): Arrangement pattern of components within material
- **MaterialLayers** (Entity): Distinct strata or levels within material structure
- **MixingPatterns** (State): Distribution patterns of components within mixture
- **BondingTypes** (Relation): Types of connections between material components
- **MaterialStructure** (State): Overall organization of material composition
- **FunctionalProperties** (Quality): Performance characteristics resulting from structure

**Relations:**

- MaterialAnalyst examines CompositeSubstances
- CompositeSubstances contain ComponentMaterials
- ComponentMaterials form MaterialLayers
- ComponentMaterials exhibit MixingPatterns
- ComponentMaterials connected through BondingTypes
- MaterialLayers and MixingPatterns create MaterialStructure
- MaterialStructure determines FunctionalProperties

### Scenario 3: Material State and Phase Changes

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Agent observes #PhaseMaterials undergoing #StateTransitions due to #EnvironmentalConditions. The process involves #EnergyApplication or #EnergyRemoval that triggers #PhaseChanges, resulting in #NewMaterialStates with different #BehavioralCharacteristics.

**Elements:**

- **Agent** (Entity): Person observing or causing material state changes
- **PhaseMaterials** (Entity): Substances capable of existing in multiple states
- **StateTransitions** (Event/Process): Changes between material phases
- **EnvironmentalConditions** (State): Temperature, pressure, and other contextual factors
- **EnergyApplication** (Event/Process): Addition of heat, pressure, or other energy forms
- **EnergyRemoval** (Event/Process): Extraction of heat or energy from material
- **PhaseChanges** (Event/Process): Transformation between solid, liquid, gas, or other states
- **NewMaterialStates** (State): Resulting material phase after transformation
- **BehavioralCharacteristics** (Quality): Properties specific to each material state

**Relations:**

- Agent observes PhaseMaterials
- EnvironmentalConditions trigger StateTransitions
- Agent applies EnergyApplication or EnergyRemoval
- EnergyApplication causes PhaseChanges
- PhaseChanges result in NewMaterialStates
- NewMaterialStates exhibit BehavioralCharacteristics
- BehavioralCharacteristics differ across material states

## Emotions and Affective States

**Domain:** Psychological  
**Subdomain:** Emotions and Affective States

### Scenario 1: Emotional Recognition and Expression

**Description:** In this scenario, an #EmotionalSubject experiences #EmotionalStates triggered by #EmotionalStimuli. The subject exhibits #EmotionalExpressions through #BodyLanguage and #VocalPatterns, which are interpreted by #SocialObservers through #EmotionalRecognition processes that may lead to #SocialResponses.


**Elements:**

- **EmotionalSubject** (Entity): Person experiencing emotional states
- **EmotionalStates** (State): Internal affective conditions like joy, anger, fear, sadness
- **EmotionalStimuli** (Entity): Events, objects, or situations that trigger emotions
- **EmotionalExpressions** (Event/Process): Outward manifestations of internal emotional states
- **BodyLanguage** (Entity): Postures, gestures, and facial expressions conveying emotion
- **VocalPatterns** (Entity): Tone, pitch, and rhythm changes expressing emotion
- **SocialObservers** (Entity): People interpreting others' emotional expressions
- **EmotionalRecognition** (Event/Process): Identifying emotions in others through observation
- **SocialResponses** (Event/Process): Behavioral reactions to perceived emotions

**Relations:**

- EmotionalStimuli trigger EmotionalStates in EmotionalSubject
- EmotionalStates generate EmotionalExpressions
- EmotionalExpressions manifest through BodyLanguage and VocalPatterns
- SocialObservers perform EmotionalRecognition of EmotionalExpressions
- EmotionalRecognition leads to SocialResponses
- SocialResponses may become EmotionalStimuli for EmotionalSubject

### Scenario 2: Emotional Regulation and Coping

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Individual experiences #IntenseEmotions that may be #Disruptive to their functioning. The Individual employs #RegulationStrategies such as #CognitiveReappraisal, #EmotionalSuppression, or #SeekingSupport to achieve #EmotionalBalance and maintain #AdaptiveFunctioning.


**Elements:**

- **Individual** (Entity): Person managing their emotional responses
- **IntenseEmotions** (State): Strong affective states that may impair functioning
- **Disruptive** (Quality): Characteristic of emotions that interfere with normal activities
- **RegulationStrategies** (Event/Process): Techniques used to modify emotional responses
- **CognitiveReappraisal** (Event/Process): Changing interpretation of emotional situations
- **EmotionalSuppression** (Event/Process): Deliberately inhibiting emotional expression
- **SeekingSupport** (Event/Process): Requesting help from others for emotional management
- **EmotionalBalance** (State): Stable, manageable level of emotional intensity
- **AdaptiveFunctioning** (State): Effective performance in daily activities despite emotions

**Relations:**

- Individual experiences IntenseEmotions
- IntenseEmotions may be Disruptive to functioning
- Individual employs RegulationStrategies to manage IntenseEmotions
- RegulationStrategies include CognitiveReappraisal, EmotionalSuppression, SeekingSupport
- Successful RegulationStrategies lead to EmotionalBalance
- EmotionalBalance enables AdaptiveFunctioning

### Scenario 3: Emotional Memory and Associations

**Description:** In this scenario, a #Person encounters #MemoryTriggers that activate #EmotionalMemories associated with #PastExperiences. This creates #EmotionalReactivation of previous feelings, which may influence current #DecisionMaking and #BehavioralChoices through #AffectiveInfluence on cognitive processes.


**Elements:**

- **Person** (Entity): Individual experiencing emotional memory activation
- **MemoryTriggers** (Entity): Environmental cues that activate emotional memories
- **EmotionalMemories** (Entity): Stored associations between experiences and emotions
- **PastExperiences** (Entity): Previous events that created emotional associations
- **EmotionalReactivation** (Event/Process): Re-experiencing emotions from previous experiences
- **DecisionMaking** (Event/Process): Current choice processes influenced by emotional memories
- **BehavioralChoices** (Entity): Actions selected based on current decision-making
- **AffectiveInfluence** (Relation): Impact of emotions on cognitive and behavioral processes

**Relations:**

- MemoryTriggers activate EmotionalMemories
- EmotionalMemories connected to PastExperiences
- EmotionalMemories create EmotionalReactivation
- EmotionalReactivation exerts AffectiveInfluence on DecisionMaking
- DecisionMaking determines BehavioralChoices
- BehavioralChoices may create new MemoryTriggers

## Reasoning and Problem-Solving

**Domain:** Cognitive  
**Subdomain:** Reasoning and Problem-Solving

### Scenario 1: Analytical Problem Decomposition

**Description:** In this scenario, a #ProblemSolver faces a #ComplexProblem and employs #DecompositionStrategies to break it into #SubProblems. The solver analyzes #ProblemComponents, identifies #LogicalRelationships, and develops #SolutionSteps that address each component systematically to achieve #ProblemResolution.

**Elements:**

- **ProblemSolver** (Entity): Person engaged in systematic problem analysis
- **ComplexProblem** (Entity): Multifaceted issue requiring systematic approach
- **DecompositionStrategies** (Event/Process): Methods for breaking problems into parts
- **SubProblems** (Entity): Component issues derived from main problem
- **ProblemComponents** (Entity): Individual elements or aspects of the problem
- **LogicalRelationships** (Relation): Connections and dependencies between problem parts
- **SolutionSteps** (Entity): Ordered actions designed to address problem components
- **ProblemResolution** (State): Successful solution of the original complex problem

**Relations:**

- ProblemSolver encounters ComplexProblem
- ProblemSolver applies DecompositionStrategies to ComplexProblem
- DecompositionStrategies create SubProblems
- SubProblems contain ProblemComponents
- ProblemComponents connected through LogicalRelationships
- ProblemSolver develops SolutionSteps for each SubProblem
- SolutionSteps lead to ProblemResolution

### Scenario 2: Hypothesis Formation and Testing

**Description:** In this scenario, a #Reasoner observes #ProblemEvidence and forms #Hypotheses to explain observations. The reasoner designs #Tests to evaluate hypotheses, collects #TestResults, and either accepts, rejects, or modifies hypotheses based on #EvidenceEvaluation leading to #TheoryRefinement.


**Elements:**

- **Reasoner** (Entity): Person engaging in hypothesis-based reasoning
- **ProblemEvidence** (Entity): Observable facts or data relevant to the problem
- **Hypotheses** (Entity): Proposed explanations for observed evidence
- **Tests** (Event/Process): Systematic procedures to evaluate hypothesis validity
- **TestResults** (Entity): Data produced by hypothesis testing procedures
- **EvidenceEvaluation** (Event/Process): Assessment of how well evidence supports hypotheses
- **TheoryRefinement** (Event/Process): Modification of explanations based on evidence

**Relations:**

- Reasoner observes ProblemEvidence
- ProblemEvidence leads to Hypotheses formation
- Reasoner designs Tests to evaluate Hypotheses
- Tests produce TestResults
- Reasoner conducts EvidenceEvaluation of TestResults
- EvidenceEvaluation leads to TheoryRefinement
- TheoryRefinement may generate new Hypotheses

### Scenario 3: Intuitive and Creative Problem Solving

**Description:** In this scenario, a #CreativeThinker encounters #IllDefinedProblems that resist systematic analysis. The thinker employs #IntuitiveProcesses, #AnalogyMaking, and #CreativeStrategies to generate #NovelSolutions through #InsightMoments that provide #BreakthroughUnderstanding.


**Elements:**

- **CreativeThinker** (Entity): Person using non-systematic problem-solving approaches
- **IllDefinedProblems** (Entity): Issues lacking clear structure or solution paths
- **IntuitiveProcesses** (Event/Process): Non-conscious cognitive operations producing insights
- **AnalogyMaking** (Event/Process): Drawing connections between different domains
- **CreativeStrategies** (Event/Process): Non-conventional approaches to problem solving
- **NovelSolutions** (Entity): Original or unexpected approaches to problem resolution
- **InsightMoments** (Event/Process): Sudden realizations of problem solutions
- **BreakthroughUnderstanding** (State): Profound comprehension that resolves the problem

**Relations:**

- CreativeThinker faces IllDefinedProblems
- IllDefinedProblems trigger IntuitiveProcesses
- CreativeThinker employs AnalogyMaking and CreativeStrategies
- AnalogyMaking connects different knowledge domains
- CreativeStrategies may lead to InsightMoments
- InsightMoments reveal NovelSolutions
- NovelSolutions provide BreakthroughUnderstanding

## Signs and Communication and Language

**Domain:** Representational  
**Subdomain:** Signs and Communication and Language

### Scenario 1: Symbolic Communication and Meaning Exchange

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MessageSender encodes #IntendedMeanings into #SymbolicMessages using #CommunicationSystems. The messages are transmitted through #CommunicationChannels to a #MessageReceiver who performs #MeaningDecoding to extract #InterpretedMeanings, which may differ from original intentions due to #SemanticNoise or #ContextualFactors.

**Elements:**

- **MessageSender** (Entity): Person encoding and transmitting symbolic messages
- **IntendedMeanings** (Entity): Concepts or information the sender wishes to communicate
- **SymbolicMessages** (Entity): Encoded representations carrying meaning
- **CommunicationSystems** (Entity): Languages, sign systems, or symbolic codes
- **CommunicationChannels** (Entity): Media through which messages are transmitted
- **MessageReceiver** (Entity): Person receiving and interpreting symbolic messages
- **MeaningDecoding** (Event/Process): Extraction of meaning from symbolic messages
- **InterpretedMeanings** (Entity): Concepts understood by the receiver
- **SemanticNoise** (Entity): Factors that distort meaning transmission
- **ContextualFactors** (State): Situational elements affecting meaning interpretation

**Relations:**

- MessageSender encodes IntendedMeanings into SymbolicMessages
- MessageSender uses CommunicationSystems for encoding
- SymbolicMessages transmitted through CommunicationChannels
- MessageReceiver receives SymbolicMessages
- MessageReceiver performs MeaningDecoding
- MeaningDecoding produces InterpretedMeanings
- SemanticNoise may distort SymbolicMessages
- ContextualFactors influence MeaningDecoding

### Scenario 2: Language Structure and Grammar

**Description:** In this scenario, a #LanguageUser combines #LinguisticUnits according to #GrammaticalRules to create #SyntacticStructures that express #SemanticContent. The process involves #WordSelection, #SentenceConstruction, and #DiscourseOrganization following #LanguageConventions to achieve #CommunicativeGoals.

**Key Cognitive Principles Demonstrated:**

- **Principle 1:** Humans understand language as rule-governed system for combining units into meaningful structures
- **Principle 2:** Universal: grammatical principles, compositional meaning; Cultural: specific language rules, discourse patterns
- **Principle 3:** Connects to Cognitive domain (rule application), Cultural domain (linguistic traditions), Social domain (communication purposes)

**Elements:**

- **LanguageUser** (Entity): Person constructing or analyzing linguistic structures
- **LinguisticUnits** (Entity): Words, morphemes, phrases, and other language components
- **GrammaticalRules** (Entity): Principles governing how linguistic units combine
- **SyntacticStructures** (Entity): Organized arrangements of linguistic units
- **SemanticContent** (Entity): Meaning expressed through syntactic structures
- **WordSelection** (Event/Process): Choosing appropriate linguistic units
- **SentenceConstruction** (Event/Process): Arranging units according to grammatical rules
- **DiscourseOrganization** (Event/Process): Structuring larger units of communication
- **LanguageConventions** (Entity): Accepted patterns of language use
- **CommunicativeGoals** (Entity): Intended outcomes of language use

**Relations:**

- LanguageUser selects LinguisticUnits through WordSelection
- LanguageUser applies GrammaticalRules to LinguisticUnits
- GrammaticalRules create SyntacticStructures
- SyntacticStructures express SemanticContent
- LanguageUser performs SentenceConstruction and DiscourseOrganization
- LanguageConventions guide language construction
- Language construction serves CommunicativeGoals

### Scenario 3: Non-Verbal and Multimodal Communication

These include #VisualCues, #GesturePatterns, #ProsodicFeatures, and #SpacialArrangements that provide #AdditionalMeaning and #EmotionalSubtext to enhance #CommunicativeEffectiveness.

**Elements:**

- **NonVerbalSignals** (Entity): Communication elements beyond spoken/written language
- **VerbalElements** (Entity): Spoken or written linguistic components
- **MultimodalMessages** (Entity): Communications combining verbal and non-verbal elements

- **SpacialArrangements** (Entity): Physical positioning and environmental setup
- **AdditionalMeaning** (Entity): Information conveyed beyond literal verbal content
- **EmotionalSubtext** (Entity): Affective undertones in communication
- **CommunicativeEffectiveness** (Quality): Degree of successful meaning transmission

**Relations:**

- Communicator combines NonVerbalSignals with VerbalElements
- Combination creates MultimodalMessages
- NonVerbalSignals include VisualCues, GesturePatterns, ProsodicFeatures, SpacialArrangements

- MultimodalMessages increase CommunicativeEffectiveness
