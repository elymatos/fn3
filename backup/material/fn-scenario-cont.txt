Here's a summary of our session, outlining the conclusions and directives for continuing our work on the Ontolexical FrameNet (FN5) project:

Our session focused on integrating the practical, application-oriented domains of FrameNet Brasil (like "Tourism," "Health," "Sports," etc.) with the foundational, ontological categories of the FN5 framework (Physical, Biological, Social, Cognitive, Representational, Space-Time, Moral).

**Key Conclusions & Directives:**

1.  **Reconciliation of Domain Types:**
    * **FN5 Domains** are **ontological categories** representing fundamental dimensions of reality (e.g., Physical, Social).
    * **FrameNet Brasil Domains** are **thematic or epistemic groupings** (e.g., Tourism, Health) that serve as practical application contexts.
    * The core strategy is to model these FrameNet Brasil domains as **Scenarios** within the FN5 framework.

2.  **Scenarios as Complex Situations:**
    * A **Scenario** is a structured, context-specific conceptual model that integrates Ontological Elements (OEs) and relations drawn from various FN5 foundational domains and subdomains.
    * This approach leverages the **Descriptions and Situations (D&S) framework** from DUL to formally structure these scenarios. A `Description` defines the abstract schema of the thematic domain, while a `Situation` represents a concrete instance of that schema.

3.  **Integration and Mapping Strategy:**
    * **Lexical Frames** from FrameNet Brasil remain language-focused and usage-based.
    * **Frame Elements (FEs)** from these lexical frames are mapped to **Ontological Elements (OEs)** defined within the FN5 Scenarios.
    * This method allows for ontological formalization of thematic domains, enhances interoperability across scenarios, and promotes the reusability of frames in different contexts.
    * Existing FrameNet Brasil frames are **not replaced**, but are **restructured and grounded** via FN5 scenarios.

4.  **LLM Task Directives (for generating scenario structures):**
    * **Define the Scenario:** Provide a brief descriptive text capturing the core cognitive schema of the thematic domain.
    * **Identify Ontological Elements (OEs):** List key entities, events, states, qualities, and relations. Classify each OE by its type (`@entity`, `@event`, `@state`, `@quality`, `@relation`) and its most fitting FN5 Ontological Domain/Subdomain. Use the `<scenario_name>:<element_name>` naming convention (e.g., `Tourism:Tourist`).
    * **Propose Core Relations:** Define essential natural language relations between OEs, indicating their type (e.g., `structural`, `causal`, `temporal`).
    * **Illustrate D&S Application:** Explain how the scenario aligns with the D&S framework, identifying its potential `Description` and example `Situations`.
    * **Suggest Lexical Frame Mappings:** Provide examples of FrameNet lexical frames and illustrate how their FEs would map to the defined OEs.
