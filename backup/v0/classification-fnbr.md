Here’s an initial draft of classification criteria for FrameNet frames, grounded in the observed Frame Elements and their descriptions:

---

### **1. Event**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - Presence of core Frame Elements like: `Agent`, `Theme`, `Goal`, `Instrument`, `Patient`, `Cause`.
        
    - Descriptions include verbs or constructions denoting **change**, **interaction**, or **occurrence**.
        
    - Often includes a `Time` or `Manner` FE as peripheral.
        
- **Typical Syntax Patterns:** Verb-headed expressions (e.g., "abandon", "destroy", "attack").
    

---

### **2. Process**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - Descriptions emphasize **temporal unfolding** or **phases** (start, ongoing, end).
        
    - May co-occur with `State` or `Event`.
        
    - FEs include `Stage`, `Duration`, `Development`, `Intermediate state`.
        
- **Typical Frames:** Lifecycle transitions, learning, maturation.
    

---

### **3. State**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - Core FEs include `State`, `Condition`, `Status`, `Situation`, `Holder`.
        
    - Descriptions refer to being in a condition over time **without inherent change**.
        
    - Often includes `Time`, but no `Goal` or `Result`.
        
- **Typical Examples:** Ownership, hunger, location.
    

---

### **4. Attribute**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - Core FEs involve properties: `Value`, `Extent`, `Degree`, `Measure`, `Quality`.
        
    - Descriptions involve static evaluative or scalar properties.
        
    - Often applies to an `Entity`, `Holder`, or `Thing`.
        
- **Example Frames:** Size_description, Riskiness, Beauty.
    

---

### **5. Relation**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - Core or peripheral FEs include spatial or logical connectors: `Location`, `Place`, `Path`, `Source`, `Goal`, `Direction`.
        
    - Descriptions refer to positioning, connection, causality, hierarchy.
        
- **Example Frames:** Containment, Cause_to_amalgamate, Temporal_collocation.
    

---

### **6. Entity**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - The frame is defined by static existence or identification of an entity.
        
    - Core FEs: `Entity`, `Object`, `Item`, `Concept`.
        
    - Often lacking dynamic roles like `Agent`, `Goal`, or `Cause`.
        
- **Rare in FrameNet** but may appear in knowledge-representation alignments.
    

---

### **7. Image Schema**

- **Key Indicators:**
    
    - FEs and descriptions invoke **topological** or **kinesthetic** primitives: `Container`, `Source`, `Path`, `Force`, `Balance`.
        
    - Underlie metaphoric or schematic structures (e.g., `Containment`, `Support`, `Link`).
        
- **Challenge:** Often implicit; may require cross-referencing with cognitive linguistics theory.
