
# Psychological Domain

## Emotions and Affective States

**Domain:** Psychological  
**Subdomain:** Emotions and Affective States

### Scenario 1: Emotional Recognition and Expression

**Description:** In this scenario, an #EmotionalSubject experiences #EmotionalStates triggered by #EmotionalStimuli. The subject exhibits #EmotionalExpressions through #BodyLanguage and #VocalPatterns, which are interpreted by #SocialObservers through #EmotionalRecognition processes that may lead to #SocialResponses.


**Elements:**

- **EmotionalSubject** (Entity): Person experiencing emotional states
- **EmotionalStates** (State): Internal affective conditions like joy, anger, fear, sadness
- **EmotionalStimuli** (Entity): Events, objects, or situations that trigger emotions
- **EmotionalExpressions** (Event/Process): Outward manifestations of internal emotional states
- **BodyLanguage** (Entity): Postures, gestures, and facial expressions conveying emotion
- **VocalPatterns** (Entity): Tone, pitch, and rhythm changes expressing emotion
- **SocialObservers** (Entity): People interpreting others' emotional expressions
- **EmotionalRecognition** (Event/Process): Identifying emotions in others through observation
- **SocialResponses** (Event/Process): Behavioral reactions to perceived emotions

**Relations:**

- EmotionalStimuli trigger EmotionalStates in EmotionalSubject
- EmotionalStates generate EmotionalExpressions
- EmotionalExpressions manifest through BodyLanguage and VocalPatterns
- SocialObservers perform EmotionalRecognition of EmotionalExpressions
- EmotionalRecognition leads to SocialResponses
- SocialResponses may become EmotionalStimuli for EmotionalSubject

### Scenario 2: Emotional Regulation and Coping

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Individual experiences #IntenseEmotions that may be #Disruptive to their functioning. The Individual employs #RegulationStrategies such as #CognitiveReappraisal, #EmotionalSuppression, or #SeekingSupport to achieve #EmotionalBalance and maintain #AdaptiveFunctioning.


**Elements:**

- **Individual** (Entity): Person managing their emotional responses
- **IntenseEmotions** (State): Strong affective states that may impair functioning
- **Disruptive** (Quality): Characteristic of emotions that interfere with normal activities
- **RegulationStrategies** (Event/Process): Techniques used to modify emotional responses
- **CognitiveReappraisal** (Event/Process): Changing interpretation of emotional situations
- **EmotionalSuppression** (Event/Process): Deliberately inhibiting emotional expression
- **SeekingSupport** (Event/Process): Requesting help from others for emotional management
- **EmotionalBalance** (State): Stable, manageable level of emotional intensity
- **AdaptiveFunctioning** (State): Effective performance in daily activities despite emotions

**Relations:**

- Individual experiences IntenseEmotions
- IntenseEmotions may be Disruptive to functioning
- Individual employs RegulationStrategies to manage IntenseEmotions
- RegulationStrategies include CognitiveReappraisal, EmotionalSuppression, SeekingSupport
- Successful RegulationStrategies lead to EmotionalBalance
- EmotionalBalance enables AdaptiveFunctioning

### Scenario 3: Emotional Memory and Associations

**Description:** In this scenario, a #Person encounters #MemoryTriggers that activate #EmotionalMemories associated with #PastExperiences. This creates #EmotionalReactivation of previous feelings, which may influence current #DecisionMaking and #BehavioralChoices through #AffectiveInfluence on cognitive processes.


**Elements:**

- **Person** (Entity): Individual experiencing emotional memory activation
- **MemoryTriggers** (Entity): Environmental cues that activate emotional memories
- **EmotionalMemories** (Entity): Stored associations between experiences and emotions
- **PastExperiences** (Entity): Previous events that created emotional associations
- **EmotionalReactivation** (Event/Process): Re-experiencing emotions from previous experiences
- **DecisionMaking** (Event/Process): Current choice processes influenced by emotional memories
- **BehavioralChoices** (Entity): Actions selected based on current decision-making
- **AffectiveInfluence** (Relation): Impact of emotions on cognitive and behavioral processes

**Relations:**

- MemoryTriggers activate EmotionalMemories
- EmotionalMemories connected to PastExperiences
- EmotionalMemories create EmotionalReactivation
- EmotionalReactivation exerts AffectiveInfluence on DecisionMaking
- DecisionMaking determines BehavioralChoices
- BehavioralChoices may create new MemoryTriggers

## Perception and Sensation

**Domain:** Psychological  
**Subdomain:** Perception and Sensation

### Scenario 1: Sensory Input Processing and Perceptual Organization

**Description:** In this scenario, a #Perceiver receives #SensoryStimuli through #SensoryChannels and engages in #PerceptualProcessing to organize sensory information into #PerceptualObjects and #SensoryPatterns. The perceiver uses #AttentionalFocus and #PerceptualFiltering to create #OrganizedExperience from complex #SensoryInput.


**Elements:**

- **Perceiver** (Entity): Person actively processing and organizing sensory information
- **SensoryStimuli** (Entity): Environmental information detected by sensory organs
- **SensoryChannels** (Entity): Different modalities for receiving information (vision, hearing, touch, etc.)
- **PerceptualProcessing** (Process): Active organization and interpretation of sensory input
- **PerceptualObjects** (Entity): Organized units of perceptual experience (objects, sounds, textures)
- **SensoryPatterns** (Entity): Recurring arrangements or sequences in sensory information
- **AttentionalFocus** (Process): Selective concentration on particular aspects of sensory input
- **PerceptualFiltering** (Process): Screening and selection of relevant sensory information
- **OrganizedExperience** (State): Coherent perceptual understanding of environmental input
- **SensoryInput** (Entity): Raw information received through sensory systems

**Relations:**

- Perceiver receives SensoryStimuli through multiple SensoryChannels
- Perceiver engages in PerceptualProcessing of SensoryInput
- PerceptualProcessing organizes stimuli into PerceptualObjects and SensoryPatterns
- AttentionalFocus directs PerceptualProcessing toward relevant information
- PerceptualFiltering eliminates irrelevant SensoryStimuli
- PerceptualProcessing creates OrganizedExperience from SensoryInput
- SensoryPatterns emerge from organized PerceptualObjects

### Scenario 2: Perceptual Thresholds and Sensitivity Variations

**Description:** In this scenario, a #SensoryMonitor experiences varying #PerceptualThresholds and #SensitivityLevels across different #SensoryModalities, recognizing #DetectionLimits and #DiscriminationAbilities. The monitor understands #SensoryAdaptation and #PerceptualChanges that affect #SensoryAwareness and #PerceptualAccuracy.


**Elements:**

- **SensoryMonitor** (Entity): Person assessing and monitoring their own sensory capabilities
- **PerceptualThresholds** (Quality): Minimum levels of stimulation required for detection
- **SensitivityLevels** (Quality): Degrees of responsiveness to sensory stimulation
- **SensoryModalities** (Entity): Different types of sensory experience (visual, auditory, tactile, etc.)
- **DetectionLimits** (Quality): Boundaries of what can be sensed or perceived
- **DiscriminationAbilities** (Quality): Capacity to distinguish between similar stimuli
- **SensoryAdaptation** (Process): Adjustment of sensitivity based on exposure levels
- **PerceptualChanges** (Process): Alterations in sensory experience over time
- **SensoryAwareness** (State): Consciousness of one's own sensory capabilities and limitations
- **PerceptualAccuracy** (Quality): Precision and reliability of sensory detection and discrimination

**Relations:**

- SensoryMonitor experiences PerceptualThresholds across SensoryModalities
- Different SensoryModalities have distinct SensitivityLevels and DetectionLimits
- SensoryMonitor assesses DiscriminationAbilities within each modality
- SensoryAdaptation causes PerceptualChanges in SensitivityLevels
- SensoryAwareness includes understanding of personal PerceptualThresholds
- PerceptualAccuracy depends on SensitivityLevels and DiscriminationAbilities
- PerceptualChanges affect overall SensoryAwareness

### Scenario 3: Perceptual Illusions and Interpretation Errors

**Description:** In this scenario, a #PerceptualExplorer encounters #PerceptualIllusions and #SensoryMisinterpretations where #SensoryEvidence conflicts with #ActualReality. The explorer investigates #PerceptualDistortions and #InterpretationErrors that reveal #PerceptualMechanisms and #CognitiveInfluences on sensory experience.


**Elements:**

- **PerceptualExplorer** (Entity): Person investigating and understanding perceptual phenomena and errors
- **PerceptualIllusions** (Entity): Systematic misperceptions that differ from physical reality
- **SensoryMisinterpretations** (Process): Incorrect understanding of sensory information
- **SensoryEvidence** (Entity): Information provided by sensory systems
- **ActualReality** (Entity): Objective physical conditions independent of perception
- **PerceptualDistortions** (Process): Systematic alterations in sensory experience
- **InterpretationErrors** (Process): Mistakes in understanding sensory information
- **PerceptualMechanisms** (Process): Underlying systems that organize sensory experience
- **CognitiveInfluences** (Entity): Mental factors that affect perceptual interpretation

**Relations:**

- PerceptualExplorer encounters PerceptualIllusions and SensoryMisinterpretations
- PerceptualIllusions create discrepancies between SensoryEvidence and ActualReality
- SensoryMisinterpretations result from PerceptualDistortions and InterpretationErrors
- PerceptualDistortions reveal underlying PerceptualMechanisms
- CognitiveInfluences contribute to InterpretationErrors
- PerceptualMechanisms can systematically alter SensoryEvidence
- InterpretationErrors demonstrate interaction between perception and cognition

## Needs and Drives and Motivations

**Domain:** Psychological  
**Subdomain:** Needs and Drives and Motivations

### Scenario 1: Basic Need Recognition and Drive Satisfaction

**Description:** In this scenario, a #MotivatedAgent experiences #BasicNeeds and #BiologicalDrives that create #MotivationalStates directing behavior toward #NeedSatisfaction. The agent recognizes #DriveSignals and engages in #GoalDirectedBehavior to achieve #DriveReduction and restore #HomeostasisBalance.


**Elements:**

- **MotivatedAgent** (Entity): Person experiencing and responding to motivational forces
- **BasicNeeds** (Entity): Fundamental requirements for survival and wellbeing (hunger, thirst, safety, etc.)
- **BiologicalDrives** (Entity): Internal forces that energize behavior toward need satisfaction
- **MotivationalStates** (State): Internal conditions that direct and energize behavior
- **NeedSatisfaction** (Process): Fulfillment of basic requirements through appropriate actions
- **DriveSignals** (Entity): Internal cues indicating need states and motivational pressure
- **GoalDirectedBehavior** (Process): Actions specifically aimed at achieving need satisfaction
- **DriveReduction** (Process): Decrease in motivational pressure following need satisfaction
- **HomeostasisBalance** (State): Optimal internal conditions maintained through need satisfaction

**Relations:**

- MotivatedAgent experiences BasicNeeds that generate BiologicalDrives
- BiologicalDrives create MotivationalStates that direct behavior
- MotivatedAgent recognizes DriveSignals indicating need states
- DriveSignals motivate GoalDirectedBehavior toward NeedSatisfaction
- NeedSatisfaction leads to DriveReduction
- DriveReduction restores HomeostasisBalance
- HomeostasisBalance reduces MotivationalStates until new needs emerge

### Scenario 2: Psychological Motivations and Achievement Drives

**Description:** In this scenario, an #Achiever pursues #PsychologicalGoals driven by #AchievementMotivation and #CompetencyNeeds. The achiever engages in #SkillDevelopment and #PerformanceImprovement while seeking #Recognition and #SelfActualization through #AccomplishmentPursuit and #MasteryOrientation.

**Elements:**

- **Achiever** (Entity): Person motivated by achievement and competency goals
- **PsychologicalGoals** (Entity): Objectives related to personal growth, competence, and self-realization
- **AchievementMotivation** (Entity): Drive to accomplish challenging tasks and reach standards of excellence
- **CompetencyNeeds** (Entity): Requirements for feeling capable and effective
- **SkillDevelopment** (Process): Improvement of abilities and expertise
- **PerformanceImprovement** (Process): Enhancement of task execution and outcomes
- **Recognition** (Entity): Acknowledgment and appreciation from others for achievements
- **SelfActualization** (Process): Realization of personal potential and authentic self-expression
- **AccomplishmentPursuit** (Process): Active efforts to achieve significant goals
- **MasteryOrientation** (Quality): Focus on learning and skill development rather than just outcomes

**Relations:**

- Achiever pursues PsychologicalGoals driven by AchievementMotivation
- AchievementMotivation emerges from CompetencyNeeds
- Achiever engages in SkillDevelopment and PerformanceImprovement
- AccomplishmentPursuit involves both SkillDevelopment and seeking Recognition
- MasteryOrientation guides approach to SkillDevelopment
- PerformanceImprovement serves both CompetencyNeeds and Recognition seeking
- SelfActualization emerges from successful AccomplishmentPursuit

### Scenario 3: Social Motivations and Relationship Drives

**Description:** In this scenario, a #SocialSeeker is motivated by #BelongingNeeds and #RelationshipDrives to form #SocialConnections and maintain #InterpersonalBonds. The seeker engages in #SocialApproachBehavior and #RelationshipBuilding while seeking #SocialAcceptance and #EmotionalIntimacy.


**Elements:**

- **SocialSeeker** (Entity): Person motivated by social connection and belonging needs
- **BelongingNeeds** (Entity): Fundamental requirements for social inclusion and group membership
- **RelationshipDrives** (Entity): Motivational forces toward forming and maintaining interpersonal connections
- **SocialConnections** (Entity): Bonds and relationships formed with other people
- **InterpersonalBonds** (Relation): Emotional and social ties between individuals
- **SocialApproachBehavior** (Process): Actions aimed at initiating social contact and connection
- **RelationshipBuilding** (Process): Development and strengthening of interpersonal bonds
- **SocialAcceptance** (Entity): Being welcomed and valued by others or groups
- **EmotionalIntimacy** (State): Deep emotional connection and closeness with others

**Relations:**

- SocialSeeker is motivated by BelongingNeeds and RelationshipDrives
- BelongingNeeds generate motivation for SocialConnections
- RelationshipDrives energize SocialApproachBehavior
- SocialApproachBehavior initiates RelationshipBuilding processes
- RelationshipBuilding creates InterpersonalBonds and SocialConnections
- SocialConnections provide SocialAcceptance
- InterpersonalBonds can develop into EmotionalIntimacy
- SocialAcceptance and EmotionalIntimacy satisfy BelongingNeeds

## Subjective Experience of the Body and Environment

**Domain:** Psychological  
**Subdomain:** Subjective Experience of the Body and Environment

### Scenario 1: Embodied Awareness and Proprioceptive Experience

**Description:** In this scenario, an #EmbodiedExperiencer maintains #BodyAwareness through #ProprioceptiveMonitoring and #InteroceptiveSensing, experiencing #SpatialEmbodiment and #BodySchemas. The experiencer integrates #PosturalInformation and #MovementSensations to maintain #EmbodiedPresence and #SomaticAwareness.

**Elements:**

- **EmbodiedExperiencer** (Entity): Person with conscious awareness of their bodily experience
- **BodyAwareness** (State): Conscious attention to bodily sensations and positioning
- **ProprioceptiveMonitoring** (Process): Sensing body position and movement through internal receptors
- **InteroceptiveSensing** (Process): Awareness of internal bodily signals and states
- **SpatialEmbodiment** (State): Sense of body position and orientation in space
- **BodySchemas** (Entity): Mental representations of body structure and capabilities
- **PosturalInformation** (Entity): Sensory data about body position and balance
- **MovementSensations** (Entity): Felt experience of bodily motion and position changes
- **EmbodiedPresence** (State): Feeling of being present in and through the body
- **SomaticAwareness** (State): Conscious attention to bodily sensations and feelings

**Relations:**

- EmbodiedExperiencer maintains BodyAwareness through ProprioceptiveMonitoring and InteroceptiveSensing
- ProprioceptiveMonitoring provides PosturalInformation and MovementSensations
- BodySchemas organize PosturalInformation into SpatialEmbodiment
- InteroceptiveSensing contributes to SomaticAwareness
- SpatialEmbodiment and SomaticAwareness create EmbodiedPresence
- MovementSensations update BodySchemas continuously
- EmbodiedPresence integrates all aspects of BodyAwareness

### Scenario 2: Environmental Presence and Atmospheric Awareness

**Description:** In this scenario, an #EnvironmentalPerceiver experiences #EnvironmentalPresence and #AtmosphericQualities through #AmbientAwareness and #SpatialSensing. The perceiver attunes to #EnvironmentalMoods and #SpatialAtmospheres while developing #PlaceSense and #EnvironmentalConnection.


**Elements:**

- **EnvironmentalPerceiver** (Entity): Person attuned to environmental qualities and atmospheric presence
- **EnvironmentalPresence** (State): Feeling of being situated within and connected to environment
- **AtmosphericQualities** (Quality): Felt characteristics of environmental spaces (warm, cold, welcoming, hostile)
- **AmbientAwareness** (Process): Perception of overall environmental context and mood
- **SpatialSensing** (Process): Awareness of spatial relationships and environmental structure
- **EnvironmentalMoods** (Quality): Emotional tones and feelings associated with places
- **SpatialAtmospheres** (Quality): Qualitative characteristics of spatial environments
- **PlaceSense** (State): Feeling of connection and belonging to specific locations
- **EnvironmentalConnection** (Relation): Bond between person and environmental setting

**Relations:**

- EnvironmentalPerceiver experiences EnvironmentalPresence through AmbientAwareness
- AmbientAwareness detects AtmosphericQualities and EnvironmentalMoods
- SpatialSensing contributes to perception of SpatialAtmospheres
- AtmosphericQualities and SpatialAtmospheres influence EnvironmentalPresence
- EnvironmentalMoods affect subjective experience of spaces
- PlaceSense emerges from positive EnvironmentalConnection
- EnvironmentalConnection strengthens through repeated EnvironmentalPresence

### Scenario 3: Embodied Emotions and Somatic Responses

**Description:** In this scenario, an #EmbodiedFeeler experiences #EmbodiedEmotions through #SomaticResponses and #BodyFeedback, recognizing #EmotionalEmbodiment and #PhysicalFeelingSates. The feeler attends to #GutFeelings and #BodyEmotions while integrating #SomaticInformation for #EmotionalAwareness.


**Elements:**

- **EmbodiedFeeler** (Entity): Person who experiences emotions through bodily sensations
- **EmbodiedEmotions** (Entity): Emotions experienced as physical sensations and bodily states
- **SomaticResponses** (Process): Physical reactions accompanying emotional states
- **BodyFeedback** (Entity): Physical sensations that provide information about emotional states
- **EmotionalEmbodiment** (State): Experience of emotions as integrated mind-body phenomena
- **PhysicalFeelingStates** (State): Bodily conditions associated with specific emotions
- **GutFeelings** (Entity): Intuitive emotional responses felt in the body
- **BodyEmotions** (Entity): Emotions primarily experienced through physical sensations
- **SomaticInformation** (Entity): Bodily data that contributes to emotional understanding
- **EmotionalAwareness** (State): Conscious recognition of emotional states through bodily experience

**Relations:**

- EmbodiedFeeler experiences EmbodiedEmotions through SomaticResponses
- SomaticResponses generate BodyFeedback about emotional states
- EmotionalEmbodiment integrates mental and PhysicalFeelingStates
- GutFeelings provide immediate SomaticInformation about situations
- BodyEmotions manifest as distinct PhysicalFeelingStates
- BodyFeedback contributes to EmotionalAwareness
- SomaticInformation enhances overall EmotionalAwareness
