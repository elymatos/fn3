## Beliefs and Knowledge and Assumptions

### Definition

This subdomain encompasses human understanding of the information, beliefs, and assumptions that constitute their knowledge base and worldview. It captures how humans conceptualize what they know, how they know it, and the various forms of knowledge and belief that guide their understanding and behavior.

### Justification

Knowledge and belief systems are fundamental to human cognition and behavior. Humans naturally distinguish between what they know with certainty versus what they believe or assume. This subdomain is essential because it represents the cognitive content that shapes all other mental processes—the background knowledge and beliefs that provide context for perception, reasoning, and action.

### Exemplary Concepts

- **Knowledge types** (factual knowledge, procedural knowledge, experiential knowledge, tacit knowledge)
- **Belief systems** (core beliefs, peripheral beliefs, religious beliefs, political beliefs, scientific beliefs)
- **Epistemic states** (certainty, doubt, confidence, skepticism, conviction, open-mindedness)
- **Knowledge sources** (experience, education, authority, intuition, tradition, evidence)
- **Assumptions** (background assumptions, default beliefs, implicit knowledge, taken-for-granted truths)
- **Belief revision** (learning, updating beliefs, changing minds, paradigm shifts, cognitive dissonance)
- **Knowledge organization** (mental models, frameworks, theories, conceptual structures)
- **Epistemic virtues** (intellectual humility, curiosity, critical thinking, open inquiry)

### Cognitive Relevance

Humans develop metacognitive awareness of their own knowledge and beliefs and can distinguish between different levels of certainty and types of knowledge. They understand how beliefs influence perception and can engage in belief revision when confronted with new evidence.

### Boundary Considerations

Distinguished from "Memory and Learning" (which focuses on acquisition processes rather than content) and "Beliefs and Worldviews" in the Cultural domain (which emphasizes shared cultural frameworks rather than individual cognitive content).
