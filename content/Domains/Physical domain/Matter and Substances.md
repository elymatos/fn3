## Matter and Substances

### Definition

This subdomain encompasses human conceptualization of the material composition of reality, including the fundamental building blocks of the physical world and their perceived properties. It captures how humans categorize and understand different types of matter based on observable characteristics, composition, and behavioral properties.

### Justification

From a cognitive perspective, humans naturally categorize the physical world into discrete substance types based on sensory experience and functional properties. This subdomain is essential because it represents the foundational level of physical reality that humans interact with directly through touch, sight, smell, and taste. It forms the basis for tool-making, cooking, construction, and countless other human activities.

### Exemplary Concepts

- **Metals** (iron, gold, copper) - characterized by hardness, conductivity, malleability
- **Liquids** (water, oil, blood) - flowing substances with varying viscosity and transparency
- **Gases** (air, steam, smoke) - invisible or visible vaporous substances
- **Organic materials** (wood, leather, bone) - substances derived from living things
- **Minerals and stones** (granite, quartz, clay) - hard, enduring earth-based materials
- **Composites** (concrete, plastic, alloys) - human-made combinations of substances
- **Powders and granules** (sand, flour, salt) - particulate forms of matter
- **Biological substances** (flesh, plant matter, bodily fluids) - matter from living systems

### Cognitive Relevance

Humans develop intuitive physics about substances through direct manipulation and observation. We form mental models about density, hardness, temperature, texture, and chemical reactivity that guide our interactions with the material world.

### Boundary Considerations

Distinguished from "Physical Transformation" (which focuses on processes of change) and "Biological Entities" (which emphasizes living systems rather than their material composition).
